ELECTROADHESION: THE FUTURE OF ANASTOMOSIS REPAIRS

Anastomatic leaks occur in about 10% of GI procedures, leading to increased costs of $10K per procedure and amount to healthcare costs of $3.6B annually in the US. Leaks increase mortality to 22% from 7% without leaks.

The standard-of-care, sutures, cannot address this dire need of alternative methods to treat anastomatic leaks. No alternative solution exist on the market which competes with sutures of staples for the required mechanical integrity.

ANASTOTAPE: SUTURE-LESS TISSUE REPAIR

How it Works: Gelectric’s adhesive patch, AnastoTape, provides a strong, safe, permanent, yet reversible adhesion to intestinal tissue. AnastoTape, is a biodegradable and biocompatible hydrogel patch shown to increase healing rate compared to standard of care of sutures.

It reduces post-operative scarring. It is applied by a simple application of a localized electric field. Adhesion is reversible by applying a localized reversible electric field.
